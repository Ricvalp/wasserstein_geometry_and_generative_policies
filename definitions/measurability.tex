\section*{Definition}

Let $(X,\mathcal A)$ and $(Y,\mathcal B)$ be measurable spaces. A map
\[
T : X \to Y
\]
is measurable if
\[
T^{-1}(B) \in \mathcal A
\]
for every $B \in \mathcal B$.

\section*{Special Case}

If $X=\mathbb R^m$ and $Y=\mathbb R^d$ are equipped with their Borel
$\sigma$-algebras, then $T:\mathbb R^m \to \mathbb R^d$ is measurable if
\[
T^{-1}(B)
\]
is a Borel subset of $\mathbb R^m$ for every Borel subset
$B \subseteq \mathbb R^d$.
