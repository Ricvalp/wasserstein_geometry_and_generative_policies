w2 between dirac masses

Let \[\mu=\delta_0, \qquad \nu=\delta_1.\] By definition, \[W_2(\mu,\nu) = \left( \inf_{\gamma\in\Pi(\mu,\nu)} \int |x-y|^2\,d\gamma(x,y) \right)^{1/2}.\]

Since all mass starts at \(0\) and must end at \(1\), the only possible transport plan is \[\gamma=\delta_{(0,1)}.\] Thus \[\int |x-y|^2\,d\gamma(x,y) = |0-1|^2 = 1.\] Therefore, \[W_2^2(\delta_0,\delta_1)=1, \qquad W_2(\delta_0,\delta_1)=\sqrt{1}=1.\]

More generally, \[W_p(\delta_a,\delta_b)=|a-b|.\]
